Atlas Freight

A three-sided freight platform where owner-operators run the show — and get paid in hours, not weeks.

OverviewThe business OS for the one-truck business

Atlas Freight connects logistics shippers and suppliers while giving owner-operator truck drivers the tools of a real back office: a loadboard that respects their time, freight management on the road, and payment that doesn’t take 30–60 days to arrive.

Most freight tech is built for brokers and fleets. Atlas starts from the driver’s seat — the smallest business on the highway — and builds the marketplace around them.

The ProblemDrive today, get paid in March

Owner-operators live in a squeeze: loadboards full of spam and re-brokered freight, paperwork scattered between a cab and a kitchen table, and invoices that pay out in weeks while fuel is due today. Factoring companies fill the gap — for a punishing cut.

Shippers and suppliers have the mirror-image problem: finding reliable capacity without a wall of middlemen between them and the person actually driving the truck.

My RoleFrom research to running code

I led UX strategy and product design across all three sides of the marketplace, then carried the design into the full-stack build. The hardest design problem wasn’t a screen — it was sequencing trust: what each side must see, prove, and escrow before money moves on its own.

ProcessDesigning for a moving cab

  • Ride along

    Interviews with owner-operators, dispatchers, and shipping managers — mapping where loads, paperwork, and payments actually stall.

  • Design the trust rails

    Modeled the arbitration engine’s states — funds committed, in transit, delivered, disputed, released — so the UI can always answer “where is my money?”

  • One platform, three dashboards

    A shared design system with three lenses: shippers post and track, suppliers coordinate, drivers find, haul, and bank.

  • Thumb-first UI

    The driver experience is designed for a phone mounted in a cab: big targets, glanceable status, and zero forms that can’t wait for a rest stop.

Key ScreensFind it, haul it, bank it

OutcomeCapacity without the middlemen

Atlas Freight gives shippers and suppliers a direct line to verified owner-operators, and gives drivers a back office that fits in a cup holder. The patented arbitration engine turns the industry’s slowest promise — “you’ll get paid” — into a state machine with a deadline.

Design for the smallest business in the system, and the marketplace gets stronger for everyone in it.
