Cookie Policy

Effective date: June 1, 2026  ·  Last updated: June 11, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Nuanced Design (nuanceddesign.com) uses cookies and similar local storage technologies on this Site. The short version: we keep this minimal.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored in your browser when you visit a website. "Similar technologies" include localStorage and sessionStorage — browser storage APIs that work like cookies but never leave your device.

What We Store

This Site stores only the following, strictly necessary items:

Name / Key Type Purpose Expires
data-theme HTML attribute / preference Remembers your dark/light mode preference so the site loads in the right theme on return visits. Session; re-detected on each visit from prefers-color-scheme
nd_fortune_shown sessionStorage Tracks whether the fortune cookie intro has already played this session to avoid repeating it. End of browser session

We do not use:

  • Advertising or tracking cookies
  • Third-party analytics cookies (no Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, etc.)
  • Cross-site tracking of any kind

Third-Party Services

Some embedded third-party services loaded on this Site may set their own cookies:

  • Calendly — if you click a scheduling link, Calendly's cookie policy applies on their domain.
  • CDN providers (jsDelivr, cdnjs) — used to load fonts and JavaScript libraries. These providers may log requests but do not set persistent cookies for tracking.

Your Choices

Because we only use strictly necessary storage, there is nothing to opt out of on our end. If you'd like to clear stored preferences:

  • Open your browser's developer tools (F12) → Application → Storage → clear localStorage and sessionStorage for nuanceddesign.com.
  • Or simply clear your browser's site data for this domain.

Your browser settings also let you block or delete all cookies globally — however, this may affect functionality on other sites.

Changes to This Policy

If our cookie usage changes, we'll update this page and the "Last updated" date above.
