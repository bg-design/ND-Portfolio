Changelog · 1994 — 2026

One designer.
Five eras.

Two decades of making things for the screen — from hand-coded pages in San Francisco to agentic builds in Binghamton, with every version of this site preserved along the way. Scroll the decades.

Web 1.0 First Experiments
1994

First hardware test 😉

Stuck a paperclip in a socket in kindergarten — my first hardware test. (Survived to tell the tale.)

1998

Dr. Brain, Thinkin’ Things & SimCity

Built and swapped parts on several computers. Learned basic programming from Castle of Dr. Brain — programming a robotic arm through a maze — and Thinkin’ Things, where you choreographed a marching band halftime show one command at a time. SimCity opened a world of emergent programming — which is why it’s the GOAT 🐐

2002

theawesome.tk

Built my first website at theawesome.tk — a curated collection of the best and coolest things on the internet, so I could share them with my friends. Because that’s what you do with websites, right? That, and crunchy cringey 90s GIF art. Also learned Photoshop along the way, and attempted to download all the world’s music and movies and burn them all to my own personal CD & DVD collection — brilliant! #agedlikemilk

Studied Landscape Architecture and Electrical Engineering — design thinking and systems thinking, side by side.

Moved to San Francisco to work for my first start-up — the Bay Area chapter begins.

Head of Operations & Web Design at PiMobility — the striking single-arch electric bicycle that hits 2000 MPGe. Ran the shop, assembled bikes, produced promo videos, and designed & built PiCycle.com from scratch.

Onward Electric Cycles

Founded Onward Electric Cycles LLC with my dad — the first company I built from scratch. We invented a hydraulic e-bike drive system that thought completely outside the electrical box, iterating until a pedal-powered pump hit 10–15 PSI.

  • Onward branding
  • Hydraulic eBike pump
2014

Every line typed by hand in San Francisco, back when that’s what “building a website” meant. jQuery, Featherlight lightboxes, and a Creative Commons badge in the footer.

Designer & EV Tech at the nation’s first carsharing company — managed a ~50-vehicle PHEV & BEV fleet, handled repairs and maintenance, and launched a pilot electric bikeshare, one of the first in the country. Also contributed web design, mobile UI, photography, and promo video.
